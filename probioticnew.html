Probiotics and COVID
Probiotics are live microorganisms which confer a health benefit on the host when administered in adequate amounts. Health benefits of probiotic bacteria include alleviation of lactose intolerance, reducing diarrhoea, regulating intestinal immunity, and improving irritable bowel syndrome.Probiotic bacteria with recognized health benefits belong mainly to the genera Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus.They are used as functional ingredients of fermentation cultures in yogurt, cheese and other fermented dairy products, but are also added to dry or frozen foods with a long shelf-life, for example milk powder, ice cream, cereals, chocolate, or candy.
